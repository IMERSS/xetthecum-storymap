Cultural Values

These species are found in the Xetthecum region on Galiano Island each have specific and varied cultural values for the Hul’q’umi’num’ speaking peoples. These cultural values may include one or more of Food, Indicator, Medicine, Material, Trade or Spiritual.

The Penelakut elders hold powerful and important sacred and spiritual knowledge regarding certain plants, animals and ceremonies of their people. This knowledge has not been included on this website; it will be passed down only to those with the rights and training to be able to receive it and use it safely and appropriately (adapted from Turner 2021 – with reference to Luciim’s Plants).

The cultural values of each species observed and documented in this mapping tool are indicated using the icons described below. Listing the cultural values in this way allows those using the site to learn about the cultural significance of each species, while respecting the sensitive nature of such information by providing minimal information.

Cultural Value: Food

These species are an important food source.
Example: the’wun - coho salmon

Cultural Value: Material

These species are used in the manufacture of items, the capture or harvesting of other species, or used as something other than as food or medicine.
Example: xpey’ – western red cedar was used to build canoes; an 800 year old cedar could be used to make four canoes.

Cultural Value: Indicator

The presence of these species indicates something, whether it be the presence of other species, a forecast (e.g. weather) or an event of spiritual significance.
Example: the ripening of lila’ – salmonberries indicates the return of Swainson’s Thrush in spring.

Cultural Value: Trade

These species were traded for other items.
Example: skw’lhey – little neck clams (and other types of clams) were used to trade for other items.

Cultural Value: Medicine

These species or portions of them are used as medicine.
Example: q’ey’xulhp – cascara

Cultural Value: Spiritual

These species are a part of spiritual ceremony or hold spiritual meaning and significance. Example: yuxwule’ – bald eagle
